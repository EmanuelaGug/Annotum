Good afternoon,

I recently came across a post about the open science movement that includes a link to your Annotum project. I found it to be very interesting and close to a research topic I am co-editing together with Dr. Kriegeskorte (cc). I am attaching the full invitation below.
___________________________________________________________
FULL INVITATION

Dear colleague,

We've noticed your contributions to the debate on scientific publishing. We would therefore like to invite you to consider a contribution to a Special Topic entitled “Beyond open access: visions for open evaluation of scientific papers by post-publication peer review”, which we are editing for Frontiers in Computational Neuroscience.

A scientific publication system needs to provide two basic services: access and evaluation.
Open access is now widely accepted as desirable and is slowly beginning to become a reality. However, the second essential element, open evaluation, has received less attention. This Special Topic in Frontiers in Computational Neuroscience will therefore collect visions for a future system of open evaluation.

We believe that this topic requires transdisciplinary expertise and the cognitive, computational, and brain sciences are best prepared to take on this task, which will involve social and psychological considerations, software design, and modeling of the networks of scientific papers and authors and their interrelationships.

A Frontiers’ Special Topic features the latest advances and challenges in hot research areas. It is a collection of at least ten articles, which may be comprised of any one of Frontiers’ article types. To participate, we would first need an abstract for your paper.

The detailed focus of this Special Topic is described at the end of this email.

For more information, please read:
http://www.frontiersin.org/computational_neuroscience (journal, article format, publication fees)
http://www.frontiersin.org/Computational%20Neuroscience/specialtopics/beyond_open_access__visions_fo/137 (call for this particular Special Topic)

In case you know someone who might be interested in contributing to this Special Topic, please feel free to forward this email. We look forward to hearing from you!

Yours sincerely,

Diana Deca and Nikolaus Kriegeskorte
Guest Associate Editors, Frontiers in Computational Neuroscience
