Hi,
My team and I are publishing the first Issue of the Journal of Sport for Development on Annotum 1.0 (we've had the site up and running for some time but only now have articles ready to publish). We have a separate system that we use for manuscript submission and review - we just use Annotum for publishing. We're struggling in the final stages to get things clean and correct.

- Because our authors are not Authors in Wordpress and we didn't want to make them become wordpress members, we've bypassed the way Annotum handles authors and used our own convention within the body of the article.
- As a result, we have to put the Abstract in the body as well, since we want authors to come first.
- This creates problems for the homepage carousel (it shows the author information when we'd rather have it show part of the abstract.
- We could put content into the abstract, but then that comes up on top of the authors, creating more problems. We can't hide the abstract section on the article using CSS because there isn't a dedicated abstract class.
- We tried creating placeholder articles (perhaps this is what's meant by "stubs" as suggested in the forum?) that link to the Article page and do have an abstract that fits in the carousel, but the main problem with this is that we then get duplicate Articles in the list of Recent Articles on the homepage (see www.jsfd.org/volumes for our placeholder homepage).

I'm busy trying to find a clean work-around. I'd like to be able to use the homepage template, but it's looking right now like the only way around might be to just set up our own homepage with links to the articles.

Would really appreciate any advice you can offer.

Thanks,

Zak Kaufman
Editor, Journal of Sport for Development
